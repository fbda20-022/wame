Lifestyle

Johannesburg is a cosmopolitan and culturally diverse city with a mixed variety of cultures and languages. A few example of ethnic groups found in Johannesburg are the Zulu, Sotho/Tswana/Pedi, Afrikaans, Xhosa etc. People came to Johannesburg for different reasons but most of them came for job opportunities in Gold mines.

Fashion and Arts

How appropriate that the meaning of Maboneng, “Place of Light”, and Johannesburg’s nickname, “City of Gold”, both refer to elements considered inspiring and beautiful that can be altered to make a new perspective visible or to bring forth a completely new creation like art.Maboneng’s “exterior” has been shaped and moulded into a visually stimulating space, as part of Propertuity’s vision to breath new life into the region, and it now boast a renewed atmosphere that inspires others to express their creativity. The region is home to a host of art galleries, stores where uniquely designed items can be purchased, museums where artistic expression is apparent and a number of graffiti walls.It goes without saying that this creative hub should be high on your travel list.

Some of the trendiest places in the area (and nearby) that are worth a visit include:

AGOG Gallery: This gallery is one of the most sought-after art spaces in the city, featuring works by local and international artists.

Apartheid Museum: Located approximately 20 minutes away, by vehicle, this museum showcases moving exhibitions about apartheid and is, further, an excellent example of design.

Madiba Mural: A gift to the city of Johannesburg, this impressive mural of Nelson Mandela in a boxing stance was created by street artist, Ricky Lee Gordon.

The best thing about the Jozi lifesttyle is how they dress, from the township pantsula attire to the glamourous designer clothes. Fashion is about expressing your identity by the way you dress and carry yourself and it is also their way of communicating.
